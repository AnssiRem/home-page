My works

Dashbot Ninja

The project I worked on during the summer 2019. Game is about dashing through robotic enemies in varied hazardous enviroments combining casual fun and hardcore action. The game took part in Assembly Summer 2019's game dev competition placing 4th!

The game is available for download on Google Play and on Steam!

I contributed game design and programming. Made with Unity.

Frog Out!

As it states on the Steam storepage: "Frog Out! is a wacky local multiplayer party game for up to 4 players." It features three different gamemodes, plenty of frog skins to choose from and wacky couch game fun.

Frog Out! is my first commercial release. I did programming in the game's development team working on live updates, mobile port and online play.

Made with Unreal Engine.

Ron Colt

The game was produced during the autumn 2019. In it you take the role of Ron Colt in a twisted stage play of the same name. Onstage mayhem and bizarre power-ups coming your way! I was wholly dedicated to designing and producing the game.

Made with Unity.

Selene

Selene is a 2.5D puzzle exploration game focused on the eviromental storytelling and exploration. The game was in the works during the spring of 2019. I worked both as the story writer and the level & puzzle designer of the game.

Made with Unity.

Bumper Balls

A game I made solo practising AI scripting and network programming in 2019.

Bumper Balls features a client-server system, AI opponents and tranquil programmer art. I've made its GitHub repo public, check it out here!

Game client made with Unity and the independent server program with just Visual Studio.

Android game

A minimalistic experiment on mobile development during spring of 2019. The game utilizes mobile phone's camera, microphone and gyroscope.

Made with Android Studio.

Pluto

A vertical slice of a turn-based RPG featuring carnage and special abilites on six tactical lanes of combat. On Pluto I spent my time programming the core combat system. Made in 2018.

Made with Unity.

Game jam games

I have partook in multiple of our cooperative's jam events that have always been a pleasant way to spend a weekend concentrating on the most fun aspects of game making.

Source maps

Almost a decade ago I got my earliest experiences of game development making maps for source games like Counter-Strike, Half-Life 2 and Garry's Mod using the Hammer Editor.

Eventually my earliest dabblings evolved into a workflow of my own, even allowing me to model my friend's lodge by heart.
